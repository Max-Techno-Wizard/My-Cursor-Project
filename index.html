What are Web Cursors

web cursers are visual indicators typically a pointer that show the users mose or touchpad on a web page and allow them to interact with elements by clicking hovering or scrolling wensites can use default cursors or customize them with different shapes colors and effects using HTML CSS and javascript to match the sites deign and provide feedback

Types of Web Cursors

  • Caret Cursor
  • Pointer Cursor
  • Text Cursor
  • Crosshair Cursor
  • Move Cursor

Caret Cursor

The Caret Cursor is the default cursor. It tells us when nothing special is happening on the page â€” no links to click, nothing to move, and nothing to highlight. It basically shows that you're just pointing at regular, non-interactive space.

Pointer Cursor

The pointer cursor is the hand-shaped cursor that appears when you move your mouse over something to show that an element is clickable such as: links, buttons, interactive items, and things that take you somewhere when clicked.

Text Cursor

The text cursor, also known as the I-beam cursor, appears when you hover over text that can be selected or edited. It indicates that you can click to place the text insertion point or highlight text for copying, cutting, or formatting.

Crosshair Cursor

The crosshair cursor is a precise cursor that looks like a plus sign or cross. It is often used in graphic design and drawing applications to help users select specific points or areas on the screen with accuracy.

Move Cursor

The move cursor, often represented by a four-headed arrow, indicates that an element can be moved. It appears when you hover over draggable items, allowing users to click and drag the element to a new location on the screen.
